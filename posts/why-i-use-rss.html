Why I Still Use RSS in 2025

Posted on August 20, 2025 by Felipe

Yes, you read that right. In an age of algorithmic feeds and AI-curated content, I still use RSS feeds like it's 2005. And you know what? It's fantastic.

For those who weren't around during RSS's heyday (or have forgotten), RSS stands for "Really Simple Syndication." It's basically a way to subscribe to websites and get updates without having to manually check each one.

Why RSS Still Rocks:

  • No Algorithm: I see everything, in chronological order. No mysterious algorithm deciding what I "should" see.
  • No Tracking: RSS feeds don't track me, analyze my behavior, or build a profile to sell to advertisers.
  • Fast & Clean: Just the content, no ads, no comments, no "related articles" rabbit holes.
  • Decentralized: No single company controls my information diet. If one site goes down, I still have hundreds of others.

My current RSS setup includes:

  • Personal blogs (like this one!)
  • Niche tech sites
  • Local news sources
  • Comic strips (yes, really)
  • Academic journals in my field

Sure, setting up RSS requires a bit more effort than just opening an app. But the payoff is huge: you get back control over your information consumption.

Maybe it's time to bring back some of the old ways. Sometimes "outdated" technology isn't outdated - it's just waiting for us to remember why it was good in the first place.
