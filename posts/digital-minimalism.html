Midnight Thoughts on Digital Minimalism

Posted on August 18, 2025 by Felipe

It's 11:47 PM and I'm lying in bed, but instead of scrolling through my phone like I used to, I'm writing this post on an old laptop with a terrible keyboard. This is progress.

Six months ago, I decided to try "digital minimalism" - basically, being more intentional about how I use technology. Not a full digital detox (I still need to work), but a conscious effort to reduce the noise.

Changes I've made:

  • Deleted social media apps from my phone
  • Set up specific times for checking email
  • Bought an actual alarm clock (goodbye, phone by the bed)
  • Started reading physical books again
  • Built this simple website instead of using platforms

Unexpected benefits:

  • I sleep better (no blue light before bed)
  • Conversations are longer and deeper
  • I notice things - birds, architecture, people's expressions
  • My attention span is slowly recovering
  • I'm less anxious about... everything

Don't get me wrong - technology is amazing. I'm literally using it right now to share these thoughts with strangers on the internet. But like any powerful tool, it's all about how you use it.

The goal isn't to become a digital hermit. It's to be intentional. To choose when and how technology serves you, instead of the other way around.

Now, if you'll excuse me, I'm going to turn off this laptop and read a book made of actual paper. Revolutionary, I know.
